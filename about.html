Bio

I received my bachelor's degree in Mathematics Education and Computer Science in 2013 from the Universidad de Los Lagos (Chile). I then obtained my master's degree under the supervision of Dr. Ricardo Oyarzúa in Applied Mathematics from the Universidad del Bío-Bío (Chile) in 2015. The topic of my master thesis was the development of a new conforming finite element method for the Boussinesq model. After obtaining my master's degree, I pursued a PhD in Applied Sciences with a focus on Mathematical Engineering from the Centro de Investigación en Ingeniería Matemática (CI²MA) at the Universidad de Concepción (Chile). I completed my PhD degree under the supervision of Dr. Manuel Solano (Universidad de Concepción) and Dr. Ricardo Oyarzúa (Universidad de Bío-Bío). My PhD project, “High-Order Mixed Methods in Continuum Mechanics”, was funded by ANID-Chile through the Doctoral Scholarships Program. Broadly, I proposed and analyzed new numerical techniques for PDEs on curved domains. I furthermore developed adaptive algorithms based on a posteriori errorestimators for problems in fluid mechanics and poroelasticity.
